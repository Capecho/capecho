Capecho's **code** is source-available under the Functional Source License
(FSL-1.1-Apache-2.0; see [`LICENSE`](LICENSE)). Capecho's **brand** is not.

"Capecho", the Capecho name, logo, and wordmark (the "Marks") are trademarks of
Capecho. Source-available licenses like the FSL cover the code's copyright,
**not** trademarks. This policy explains what that means in practice, so you can
fork and build with confidence.

## You may, without asking

- Use, self-host, and modify the code under the FSL for any Permitted Purpose —
  anything except shipping a product or service that competes with Capecho. Two
  years after each release, that release is also available under Apache-2.0.
- Run your own build for yourself or your organization.
- Refer to Capecho factually — "based on Capecho", "compatible with Capecho",
  "a fork of Capecho" — as long as you don't imply that Capecho endorses or
  produces your version.

## Please do not, without written permission

- Use the Marks as the name of your fork, product, app, or service, or in a way
  that suggests it **is** Capecho or is official/endorsed.
- Use the Capecho logo or wordmark as your own app icon or brand.
- Publish your fork to an app store, package registry, or domain under a name or
  identity that could be confused with Capecho.
- Point your build at Capecho's official backend (`api.capecho.com`) or other
  Capecho-operated infrastructure, or imply affiliation with it.

If you distribute a modified version, give it your own name and make clear it is
not the official Capecho.

## Why

The code is not the moat — the corpus, the hosted service, and the brand are.
Keeping the Marks reserved is what lets the code be fully auditable without
letting a rebranded clone trade on users' trust in the name. Questions, or want
to use the Marks for something not covered here? Email trademarks@capecho.com.
